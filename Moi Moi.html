Moi Moi

Description

Moi Moi, also known as Moin Moin, is a popular Nigerian dish made from steamed bean pudding. It's a staple in Nigerian cuisine and is often enjoyed as a main dish or a side dish. The primary ingredient is peeled black-eyed peas or brown beans, which are blended into a smooth paste and mixed with a variety of seasonings and ingredients such as onions, bell peppers, and oil.

Additional optional ingredients can include fish, boiled eggs, crayfish, or corned beef for added flavor and protein. Moi Moi is usually wrapped in banana leaves, aluminum foil, or special Moi Moi containers and then steamed until it solidifies into a pudding-like texture.

Ingredients

  • Peeled black-eyed peas or brown beans
  • Onions
  • Bell peppers
  • Vegetable oil or palm oil
  • Salt
  • Water
  • Optional ingredients:
    • Fish
    • Boiled eggs
    • Crayfish
    • Corned beef
    • Other Proteins
      • Pork
      • Mutton

Steps

  1. Soak the beans in water for several hours, then peel off the skins.
  2. Blend the peeled beans with onions, bell peppers, and water to form a smooth paste.
  3. Transfer the paste to a bowl and add vegetable oil or palm oil, salt, and any optional ingredients (fish, boiled eggs, crayfish, or corned beef).
  4. Mix the ingredients thoroughly until well combined.
  5. Portion the mixture into banana leaves, aluminum foil, or special Moi Moi containers.
  6. Steam the wrapped mixture in a pot for about 45 minutes to 1 hour, or until the Moi Moi solidifies.
  7. Remove from heat and allow to cool slightly before serving.
