Akara

Description

Akara, also known as bean cakes or bean fritters, is a popular Nigerian snack made from deep-fried bean paste. It is a favorite street food and breakfast item enjoyed across Nigeria. The primary ingredient is peeled black-eyed peas or brown beans, which are ground into a smooth paste and mixed with seasonings such as onions, bell peppers, and spices.

Akara is known for its crispy exterior and soft, fluffy interior. It is often served hot and can be enjoyed on its own, with bread, or alongside pap (a fermented corn pudding). This delicious and protein-rich snack is a staple in many Nigerian households and gatherings.

Ingredients

  • Peeled black-eyed peas or brown beans
  • Onions
  • Bell peppers
  • Scotch bonnet peppers (optional for heat)
  • Salt
  • Water
  • Vegetable oil (for frying)
  • Optional ingredients:
    • Garlic
    • Crayfish

Preparation Steps

  1. Soak the beans in water for several hours, then peel off the skins.
  2. Blend the peeled beans with onions, bell peppers, scotch bonnet peppers (if using), and a little water to form a smooth paste.
  3. Transfer the paste to a bowl and add salt and any optional ingredients (garlic, crayfish). Mix thoroughly until well combined.
  4. Heat vegetable oil in a deep pan or pot over medium-high heat until hot.
  5. Using a spoon, scoop small portions of the bean paste into the hot oil, frying in batches to avoid overcrowding.
  6. Fry the akara until golden brown and crispy on the outside, about 2-3 minutes per side.
  7. Remove the akara from the oil using a slotted spoon and drain on paper towels to remove excess oil.
  8. Serve hot, either on its own, with bread, or alongside pap.
