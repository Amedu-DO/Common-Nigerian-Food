Beans

Description

Beans Porridge, also known as Ewa Oloyin or simply Beans, is a popular Nigerian dish made with beans cooked to a creamy consistency. This hearty and nutritious meal is often enjoyed for its rich flavor and satisfying texture. It is commonly prepared with honey beans (Ewa Oloyin) or black-eyed peas, which are cooked with ingredients such as onions, palm oil, and seasonings.

Beans Porridge can be enjoyed on its own or paired with fried plantains, yams, or bread. It is a versatile dish that can be adapted with additional ingredients like fish, meat, or vegetables to enhance its flavor and nutritional value.

Ingredients

  • Honey beans (Ewa Oloyin) or black-eyed peas
  • Palm oil
  • Onions
  • Garlic (optional)
  • Crayfish (optional)
  • Seasonings (salt, bouillon cubes)
  • Water
  • Optional ingredients:
    • Fish (smoked or dried)
    • Meat (beef, goat meat)
    • Vegetables (spinach, ugu)

Preparation Steps

  1. Pick and rinse the beans thoroughly to remove any dirt or stones.
  2. In a large pot, add the beans and enough water to cover them. Bring to a boil and cook until the beans start to soften.
  3. Drain the beans and add fresh water. Continue cooking until the beans are tender.
  4. In a separate pan, heat palm oil over medium heat and sauté the chopped onions (and garlic if using) until fragrant.
  5. Add the sautéed onions, palm oil, crayfish (if using), and seasonings to the pot of beans. Stir well to combine.
  6. If using fish or meat, add them to the pot and continue to cook on low heat until the flavors meld and the beans reach a creamy consistency.
  7. If using vegetables, add them during the last few minutes of cooking to ensure they are tender but still vibrant.
  8. Adjust the seasoning with salt to taste. Serve hot, optionally with fried plantains, yams, or bread.
