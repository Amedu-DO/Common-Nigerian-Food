Ogbono Soup

Description

Ogbono Soup is a popular Nigerian soup made from ground Ogbono seeds (wild mango seeds). It is a favorite in many Nigerian households and is known for its rich, savory flavor and slightly slimy texture. The soup is typically prepared with a variety of meats such as beef, goat meat, and assorted offals, as well as fish and seafood like stockfish and dried fish.

Ogbono Soup is often cooked with leafy vegetables like spinach, bitterleaf, or ugu (fluted pumpkin leaves) and is flavored with ingredients such as crayfish, palm oil, and seasonings. It is commonly served with fufu, pounded yam, or other Nigerian swallows (starchy side dishes).

Ingredients

  • Ground Ogbono seeds (wild mango seeds)
  • Palm oil
  • Assorted meats (beef, goat meat, offals)
  • Fish and seafood (stockfish, dried fish)
  • Crayfish
  • Seasonings (salt, pepper, bouillon cubes)
  • Leafy vegetables (spinach, bitterleaf, ugu)
  • Water or meat stock

Preparation Steps

  1. Boil the assorted meats and fish with seasonings until tender. Set aside the meat and stock.
  2. Heat palm oil in a pot over medium heat and add the ground Ogbono seeds. Stir continuously until the Ogbono is well incorporated and begins to thicken.
  3. Gradually add the meat stock while stirring to prevent lumps from forming.
  4. Add the cooked meats, fish, and crayfish to the pot. Stir well to combine.
  5. Simmer the soup on low heat, stirring occasionally to prevent burning.
  6. Add the leafy vegetables and continue to cook for a few more minutes until the vegetables are tender.
  7. Adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper to taste.
  8. Serve hot with fufu, pounded yam, or any preferred Nigerian swallow.
